Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 2026

At Liam Designs, we are committed to protecting your privacy and keeping your personal information secure. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use and protect your data when you visit our website or use our services. By using this website, you agree to the practices described in this policy.

1. Information We Collect

Information You Provide

When you contact us or request services, we may collect:

  • Your name
  • Email address
  • Business name and industry
  • Project details and requirements
  • Any other information you choose to provide in your message

Contact form submissions are processed by Formspree, a third-party service that securely delivers your message to our inbox. Formspree may temporarily store your details for delivery and logging purposes.

Automatically Collected Information

We do not use analytics tools, tracking scripts, or cookies to collect data about visitors to this website. No usage data is collected automatically when you visit this site.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • Service delivery: To respond to your enquiries, provide quotes and deliver web design services.
  • Communication: To send project updates, respond to questions and provide customer support.
  • Legal compliance: To comply with applicable laws and regulations where required.

3. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

This website does not use cookies, analytics tools, or any tracking technologies. We do not collect browsing data, run advertising scripts, or use any third-party tracking services.

The only data we receive is what you choose to send us directly through the contact form.

4. Data Security

We use appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal information against unauthorised access, alteration, disclosure or destruction. Access to contact form submissions is limited to those who need it in order to respond to you.

5. Your Rights

Under UK data protection law, including UK GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Access: Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Rectification: Request correction of any inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Erasure: Request deletion of your personal data in certain circumstances.
  • Restriction: Request that we restrict the processing of your data in certain cases.
  • Portability: Request transfer of your data to another service provider where technically possible.
  • Objection: Object to certain types of processing, where we are relying on legitimate interests.
